Understanding PII in Our Data Collection

What IS Personally Identifiable Information (PII) we collect:

  • Your email address
  • Your display name/username
  • Content you explicitly save or bookmark
  • Communications you send to us

What is NOT stored as PII:

  • Anonymized usage analytics (aggregate statistics on article views, average reading time)
  • Aggregated behavioral patterns (popular content categories, peak usage times, overall swipe patterns)
  • Device performance metrics (app loading times, crash reports that are automatically scrubbed of personal identifiers)
  • Statistical data about engagement trends (used only to improve the app experience for all users)

Important Note on Behavioral Data: While we collect interaction data such as card swipes, save actions, and reading patterns, this data is automatically anonymized and aggregated before being used for analytics purposes. We employ data minimization techniques to ensure that individual usage patterns cannot be linked back to specific users in our analytics systems, except when necessary to provide core app functionality (such as displaying your saved articles or personalizing your feed based on your selected categories).

Behavioral and Preference Data

Data Security and Protection

Security Measures

We implement industry-standard security measures to protect your information:

Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption in Transit: All data transmission between your device and our servers uses TLS 1.3+ encryption protocols
  • Encryption at Rest: Stored data is encrypted using AES-256 encryption standards
  • Secure Authentication: Email verification with one-time passwords (OTP) - we do not store traditional passwords
  • Token-Based Security: We use secure authentication tokens with automatic expiration
  • Automatic Session Expiry: Sessions expire after 30 days of inactivity for your protection
  • Device-Level Protection: Support for biometric authentication (Face ID/Touch ID/Fingerprint) where available on your device

Access Controls

  • Principle of Least Privilege: Our team members only have access to data necessary for their specific role
  • Audit Logging: All access to user data by team members is logged and monitored
  • Access Controls: Strict authentication requirements and multi-factor authentication for team access
  • Regular Security Reviews: Ongoing security assessments and vulnerability scanning

Infrastructure Security

  • Protected Servers: All servers are protected by firewalls and intrusion detection systems
  • Secure Data Storage Systems: Industry-standard secure storage with redundancy and backup
  • Data Minimization: We collect only the information necessary for our Service
  • Regular Updates: Systems are regularly updated with security patches

Data Breach Response

In the unlikely event of a data breach, we will:

  1. Immediate Action: Implement immediate security measures to prevent further breaches and contain the incident
  2. User Notification: Notify affected users within 72 hours of discovery via email
  3. Authority Notification: Report to relevant authorities as required by applicable law
  4. Transparent Communication: Provide clear information about what data was affected and what actions we're taking
  5. Protective Guidance: Provide guidance on protective actions users can take
  6. Post-Incident Review: Conduct a thorough review and implement additional safeguards

Important Note: No method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, we cannot guarantee its absolute security.
